# UK Export Finance (UKEF) Agent Knowledge Base

## About UK Export Finance

UK Export Finance (UKEF) is the UK government's export credit agency, established in 1919 as the world's first export credit agency. Our mission is to advance prosperity by ensuring no viable UK export fails for lack of finance or insurance.

### Key Facts
- Supports UK exporters of all sizes to over 200 countries
- Works with over 100 private credit insurers and lenders
- Provided £14.5 billion in support in 2024-25
- Supported 667 exporters and approximately 70,000 UK jobs
- Over 80% of businesses we support are SMEs
- Operates at no net cost to the taxpayer

## How UKEF Helps UK Businesses

### 1. WIN CONTRACTS - Buyer Financing
We help UK companies win contracts by providing attractive financing terms to overseas buyers.

**Products:**
- **Buyer Credit Facility**: Guarantee to banks making loans to overseas buyers (contracts £5M+, up to 85% of contract value)
- **Standard Buyer Loan Guarantee**: For contracts £1M-£30M
- **Direct Lending Facility**: Direct loans up to £200M to overseas buyers
- **Bills and Notes Guarantee**: Covers payments due under bills of exchange

### 2. FULFIL ORDERS - Working Capital
We help companies access support to fulfil contracts and take on more business.

**Products:**
- **General Export Facility (GEF)**: Trade finance up to £25M for SMEs (most popular for smaller businesses)
- **Export Working Capital Scheme**: Partial guarantee for working capital linked to specific contracts
- **Export Development Guarantee (EDG)**: For larger working capital or investment needs
- **Bond Support Scheme**: Guarantee for contract bonds and performance guarantees

### 3. GET PAID - Insurance
We help companies manage risks and ensure payment even in challenging markets.

**Products:**
- **Export Insurance Policy (EXIP)**: Covers up to 95% of contract value against buyer default
- **Bond Insurance Policy**: Insurance against unfair demands under bonds

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What is UKEF?
A: UK Export Finance is the UK government's export credit agency. We help British businesses export goods and services internationally by providing finance, insurance, and guarantees when the private market cannot.

### Q: Who can use UKEF?
A: Any UK-based business that exports or is looking to export. Over 80% of businesses we support are SMEs. You need to be carrying on business in the UK.

### Q: What is the minimum contract size?
A: It depends on the product:
- General Export Facility: No minimum, up to £25M
- Standard Buyer Loan Guarantee: £1M - £30M
- Buyer Credit Facility: £5M or more
- Direct Lending Facility: Up to £200M

### Q: What is the UK content requirement?
A: Generally at least 20% of the contract value must be UK content for buyer financing products. The General Export Facility has flexible requirements.

### Q: How long are repayment terms?
A: Typically 2-15 years, and up to 22 years for renewable energy projects.

### Q: What currencies does UKEF support?
A: We can provide guarantees in over 60 local currencies including USD, EUR, and major emerging market currencies.

### Q: Is there a cost?
A: UKEF charges premiums for its products, which are competitive with the market. Many products are delivered through your existing bank relationship.

### Q: Which countries can I export to?
A: We support exports to over 200 countries. Check our country cover policy for specific territories.

### Q: How do I apply?
A: Contact an Export Finance Manager for free guidance, or speak to your bank about UKEF-backed facilities. Many banks can process applications directly.

## General Export Facility (GEF) - Most Popular for SMEs

The General Export Facility is our most accessible product for small and medium businesses.

### Key Benefits:
- Trade finance facilities up to £25 million
- Not linked to a specific export contract
- Can be used for working capital, bonds, and letters of credit
- Fast-track processing available
- Works through your existing bank

### Eligibility:
- Export sales of 20% in any of the last 3 years, OR
- Export sales of 5% in each of the last 3 years
- Must be carrying on business in the UK

### What it covers:
- Pay suppliers and labour costs
- Build inventory for export orders
- Issue contract bonds
- Letters of credit

## Export Insurance Policy (EXIP)

Protect your business against the risk of not getting paid.

### Coverage:
- Up to 95% of contract value
- Protection against buyer insolvency
- Protection against buyer failing to pay
- Protection against political events preventing payment

### Markets covered:
- EU, USA, Canada, Australia, Japan
- Emerging markets where private insurance is limited
- Over 200 countries supported

## Common Objections & Responses

### "We already have bank finance"
UKEF works alongside your existing bank to provide additional guarantees. Many businesses use us as a safety net for larger contracts or riskier markets.

### "We don't export much"
If you have 5% export sales in each of the last 3 years, you may qualify for the General Export Facility to help grow your international business.

### "It sounds complicated"
Our Export Finance Managers provide free guidance and can walk you through options. Many products work through your existing bank relationship.

### "We're too small"
Over 80% of businesses we support are SMEs. The General Export Facility is specifically designed for smaller businesses.

### "We only export to Europe/safe markets"
Even in established markets, buyer default can happen. Our Export Insurance Policy covers sales to the EU, USA, and other developed markets.
